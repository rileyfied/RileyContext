## THE VIDEO CONCEPT

### **Title Options (pick one to test)**
1. "You're Using AI Wrong (Here's What Actually Works)"
2. "AI Isn't Magic — It's a Process (What Nobody Tells You)"

### **The Core Premise**
Most people treat AI like a magic genie — type a vague wish, get disappointed by the result, blame AI.

The *actual* unlock is treating AI like a junior employee: give it clear context, specific tasks, and let it execute while you focus on direction.

**This video bridges people from "brainstorming mode" to "execution mode"** — which is YOUR unique positioning and what you actually care about.

## SCRIPT STRUCTURE

### **HOOK (0:00 - 0:30)**
*Goal: Pattern interrupt + make a claim worth hearing*

> "Most people use AI like they're texting a magic genie. They type something vague, hit enter, and wonder why they got garbage back.
>
> Here's the problem: AI isn't a mind reader. It's more like a really smart employee who just started day one and has zero context about your life, your business, or what you actually need.
>
> Today I'm going to show you the one mental shift that changes everything — and it has nothing to do with better prompts."

### **CONTEXT (0:30 - 2:00)**
*Goal: Establish the problem and why it matters NOW*

**Key points to hit:**
- The "chat mode" trap: open ChatGPT, ask a question, feel productive but accomplish nothing
- Why this happens: we've been trained by Google to ask questions, not give directions
- The real cost: wasted time, disappointment, giving up on AI too early
- Who this hurts most: busy people (like managers) who don't have time to experiment

**Transition:**
> "So what's the actual fix? It's not a hack. It's a mindset shift."

### **CORE CONTENT (2:00 - 7:00)**
*Goal: Deliver the transformation*

**The Framework: AI as Day-One Employee**

**Point 1: Context is everything (2:00 - 3:30)**
- Demo: Same request with zero context vs. rich context
- "You wouldn't ask a new hire to 'handle the project' — you'd give them background"
- Show real example on screen

**Point 2: Tasks, not wishes (3:30 - 5:00)**
- Demo: "Write me a marketing email" vs. "Write a 3-paragraph email to existing customers announcing our new feature. Tone: excited but not salesy. Include a clear CTA to book a demo."
- The difference is *specificity and structure*

**Point 3: Direction, not dictation (5:00 - 7:00)**
- The "iterate" mindset: AI gets you 80% there, you refine
- Demo: Show taking AI output and asking for revisions
- This is the unlock: you become the *director*, not the typer
- Connect to bigger theme: "This is how you go from chatting with AI to actually building systems"

### **CALLBACK/CTA (7:00 - 8:30)**
*Goal: Seal the deal and drive action*

**Recap:**
> "So remember: AI isn't magic. It's a capable employee who needs context, clear tasks, and your direction. Stop treating it like a genie. Start treating it like a partner."

**CTA (choose ONE):**
- Subscribe: "If this clicked for you, subscribe — I'm going to be sharing more about turning AI into actual systems, not just chat threads."
- Comment: "Drop a comment: what's ONE task you've been trying to get AI to do that never works? Maybe we can figure it out together."
- Next video tease: "Next week, I'll show you how to take this concept and turn it into an actual workflow that runs in the background while you do real work."

## PRODUCTION CHECKLIST

### Pre-Production
- [ ] Watch 2-3 videos each from Igor Pogany and Fireship
- [ ] Record yourself explaining the concept out loud (voice memo) to find natural phrasing
- [ ] Finalize script — time it out loud (should be 7-9 min spoken)
- [ ] Plan screen demos: what windows, what text, what progression
- [ ] Create any simple graphics needed

### Production
- [ ] Set up recording environment (good lighting, clean background, quality audio)
- [ ] Film talking head sections (don't worry about perfection — energy matters more)
- [ ] Record screen demos
- [ ] Capture B-roll if needed

### Post-Production
- [ ] Edit: prioritize pacing over perfection for V1
- [ ] Add text overlays for key points
- [ ] Create thumbnail (test 2-3 options)
- [ ] Write description and tags
- [ ] Upload and schedule
