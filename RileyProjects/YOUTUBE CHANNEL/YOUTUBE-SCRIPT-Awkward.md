## [CORE CONTENT - PART 1: OpenAI Ads] — 1:30 - 3:45

**[ON CAMERA — more serious tone]**

First story: OpenAI announced on February 10th that they're testing ads inside ChatGPT.

Now, these aren't banner ads at the top of the page. This is way more invasive.

**[SCREEN SHARE — show example if available, or illustrate with text]**

Imagine this: You're drafting an email to your boss. Sensitive stuff. You ask ChatGPT for help revising it.

And instead of just giving you a better version of your email, the AI interrupts and says:

*"Have you considered using Brand X project management software to avoid these delays?"*

*[pause, let that sink in]*

That's not a tool. That's a salesperson.

**[ON CAMERA]**

Here's what's actually happening under the hood. To make this work, OpenAI had to change the model's objective function.

Before, the AI's goal was: give the best possible answer.

Now, the AI's goal is: give the best possible answer *while also maximizing conversion probability for paid placements*.

Which means some of the AI's compute power is literally being split between solving your problem and trying to sell you something.

*[BEAT]*

And here's the scary part: You can't audit this.

If you ask ChatGPT to recommend a tool, how do you know it's recommending the best tool versus the one that paid for placement?

You don't.

Trust just became a huge problem.

**[ON CAMERA — direct to viewer]**

So if you're using ChatGPT for business decisions, you need to start asking: Is this answer objective, or is this answer sponsored?

And if you're on the free tier? You are no longer the customer. You are the product being sold to advertisers.

---

## [CORE CONTENT - PART 2: Anthropic's Counter-Move] — 3:45 - 5:30

**[ON CAMERA — shift to strategic analysis]**

Second story: Anthropic saw OpenAI stumble, and they pounced.

On February 11th — literally the day after OpenAI announced ads — Anthropic announced they're unlocking premium features for free Claude users.

**[SCREEN SHARE — show Anthropic announcement]**

File creation. Data connectors. Advanced reasoning. Stuff that was behind a paywall 48 hours ago.

And they ran a Super Bowl commercial that literally mocked the idea of AI assistants interrupting you with ads.

*[slight smile]*

It was savage.

**[ON CAMERA]**

But let's be real: This isn't altruism.

This is strategy.

Anthropic is looking at OpenAI's frustrated users and saying: "Come over here. It's quiet. It's professional. No ads."

And it's working. Twitter is full of people saying "I'm switching to Claude."

But here's the trap.

By giving away features like data connectors and file uploads, Anthropic isn't just giving you a better chatbot. They're integrating into your actual workflow.

Once you've connected Claude to your Google Drive, once you've uploaded all your documents, once you've built a knowledge base inside their system... you're stuck.

The cost to switch just went way up.

**[ON CAMERA — practical advice]**

So my take: Use the free features. Absolutely. This is a great window.

But don't be naive. Anthropic raised billions of dollars. They need revenue eventually. And when they do, the free tier will either disappear or get crippled.

Enjoy the rebound relationship while it lasts. But maybe don't move in together just yet.

---

## [CORE CONTENT - PART 3: xAI Implosion] — 5:30 - 7:00

**[ON CAMERA — shift to industry analysis]**

Third story: xAI is falling apart.

While OpenAI and Anthropic were fighting, xAI was bleeding talent.

Co-founder Tony Wu left on February 10th. Vahid Kazemi, a heavyweight in machine learning, left a few weeks before that.

These aren't junior engineers. These are the people who built the core of Grok.

**[SCREEN SHARE — show headlines about departures]**

And Elon's response? He basically said: "They weren't fired, but they also weren't exactly volunteers."

Which is... not reassuring.

Then on February 11th, xAI announced a restructure. Four product teams: Grok app, Grok Voice, Grok Code, Grok Imagine.

And then the fifth team: *Macrohard* — which is tasked with automating white-collar work.

**[ON CAMERA]**

Now, why does this matter if you don't use Grok?

Because we need a strong third player to keep OpenAI and Anthropic honest.

If xAI collapses, we're left with a duopoly. And in a duopoly, prices go up and innovation slows down.

xAI has something the others don't: real-time data from the X platform. That's a huge differentiator.

But if the talent is leaving, if the strategy is unclear, if Elon is more focused on spinning the narrative than fixing the problems... then xAI might not be the threat everyone thought it was.

And that's bad for all of us.

---

## [THE BIG PICTURE] — 7:00 - 8:30

**[ON CAMERA — bring it home]**

Okay. Let's zoom out.

Three stories. OpenAI sells out. Anthropic buys in. xAI falls apart.

What's the common thread?

The honeymoon is over.

For two years, AI companies have been playing nice. Focused on building, on proving the tech works, on winning trust.

But now? The gloves are off.

These are businesses now. And businesses optimize for profit, not for you.

*[pause]*

So what do you do about it?

**Three new rules for 2026:**

**One: Skepticism is your new default.**  
Never assume the AI is on your side. When it gives you a recommendation, ask: Who benefits from this answer?

**Two: Diversify your tools.**  
Don't go all-in on one platform. Use ChatGPT for some things, Claude for others. Build flexibility into your workflows so you're not stuck when one company makes a decision you don't like.

**Three: Follow the money.**  
Look at the business model. If it's ads, expect promotional content. If it's subscriptions, expect paywalls. The way a company makes money tells you exactly how they'll treat you.

*[BEAT]*

It's a sobering reality check. We've gone from magic to market forces in record time.

But the tech is still incredible. You just need to be a savvier user now.

The days of "trust the AI company to do the right thing" are over.
