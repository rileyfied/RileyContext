# 🎓 CREATOR STUDY GUIDE

## PRIMARY STUDY: Topic & Teaching Style

### 1. THE AI ADVANTAGE (Igor Pogany)
**Channel:** youtube.com/@TheAIAdvantage  
**Subscribers:** 260K+  
**Why study him:** He's THE model for "AI for non-tech people"

#### Videos to Watch (in order):
1. **His most viewed ChatGPT tutorial** — Notice how he assumes intelligence but not expertise
2. **Any "Custom Instructions" video** — See how he breaks complex concepts into steps
3. **A recent tool demo** — Watch his pacing and energy

#### What to STEAL:
- His calm, patient delivery that never talks down to viewers
- How he shows, then explains, then recaps
- The way he makes complex things feel accessible
- His "here's what this means for YOU" framing

#### What to AVOID:
- His videos can feel like clean tutorials — you want more personality/opinion
- He doesn't have a strong "thesis" per video — yours should

---

### 2. MATT WOLFE
**Channel:** youtube.com/@maboroshi  
**Subscribers:** 694K+  
**Why study him:** Shows AI tools with warmth and enthusiasm

#### Videos to Watch:
1. **Any "AI News" weekly roundup** — Study his thumbnail style and hook structure
2. **A deep dive tool review** — Notice how he balances breadth and depth
3. **His Claude Computer Use video** (recent)

#### What to STEAL:
- His genuine enthusiasm (not hype, just excited curiosity)
- The "let me show you something cool" energy
- His professional but approachable vibe
- His consistency (he built an empire on showing up)

#### What to AVOID:
- His format is "news/roundup" — yours should be more thesis-driven
- He sometimes goes broad instead of deep — you want focused episodes

## SECONDARY STUDY: Style & Energy

### 3. FIRESHIP
**Channel:** youtube.com/@Fireship  
**Subscribers:** 3M+  
**Why study him:** Master of tight, punchy, opinionated content

#### Videos to Watch:
1. **ANY "in 100 seconds" video** — Pure efficiency
2. **A longer rant/opinion video** — Notice structure
3. **"God-Tier Developer Roadmap"** — See how he uses visuals

#### What to STEAL:
- **Pacing**: Every second earns its place
- **Strong opinions stated clearly**: He doesn't hedge
- **Visual humor**: Text on screen, quick cuts, memes
- **Problem-first structure**: He starts with what's wrong

#### What to AVOID:
- His audience is developers — yours isn't
- His sardonic/ironic tone can alienate casual viewers
- Don't copy his specific visual style (you'll look like a knockoff)

#### SPECIFIC TECHNIQUE TO PRACTICE:
Write your script, then cut 20% of the words. Then cut 10% more. That's Fireship energy.

### 4. HOWFINITY
**Channel:** youtube.com/@Howfinity  
**Subscribers:** 1M+  
**Why study him:** The "solve one problem completely" model

#### Videos to Watch:
1. **Any tutorial under 5 minutes**

Your "AI for managers" videos could follow this format: "How to use ChatGPT to summarize your inbox in 2 minutes"

## THE STUDY PLAN

### Before filming Video #1:

**Day 1: Igor Pogany Deep Dive**
- Watch 2-3 videos
- Take notes on: pacing, how he introduces topics, how he ends videos
- Practice explaining the "Day-One Employee" concept out loud in his style

**Day 2: Fireship Study**
- Watch 3 videos (mix of short and long)
- Study his text overlays and visual rhythm
- Write 3 different versions of your hook, each tighter than the last

**Day 3: Matt Wolfe + Howfinity**
- Watch 1-2 from each
- Notice the difference between "enthusiasm" and "clarity"
- Decide where YOUR style sits on that spectrum

**Day 4: Practice Film**
- Film yourself delivering the hook and first section
- Watch it back (this is painful but essential)
- Note what feels natural vs. forced
- Do NOT aim for perfect — aim for authentic

## YOUR UNIQUE ANGLE

After studying these creators, remember what makes YOU different:

| Other AI YouTubers | YOUR Positioning |
|-------------------|------------------|
| Show cool AI tricks | Show the **mental models** behind using AI |
| Target tech enthusiasts | Target **non-tech people** who are skeptical |
| Focus on tools | Focus on **systems and workflows** |
| Demo features | Explain the **"why" and "how to think"** |
| General business audience | **Frontline managers** with real jobs |

**Your Mantra:**
"I help non-tech people go from AI chat threads to actual systems and automation."

Every creator you study is great — but none of them own THIS position. That's your lane.
