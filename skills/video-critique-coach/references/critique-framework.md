# Critique Framework

Detailed evaluation criteria for each analysis dimension. Use this as a checklist when reviewing videos.

---

## Content & Strategy

### Topic Selection
| Rating | Indicators |
|--------|------------|
| Strong | Timely, audience-aligned, searchable, fills a gap |
| Weak | Oversaturated, off-brand, no clear audience need |

**Questions to ask:**
- Would this topic work as well in 6 months? (Evergreen vs. trending)
- Is there search demand or social conversation around this?
- Does this fit the channel's established territory?

### Angle/Hook
| Rating | Indicators |
|--------|------------|
| Strong | Clear promise, unique perspective, curiosity gap |
| Weak | Generic framing, unclear benefit, clickbait without substance |

**Questions to ask:**
- In one sentence, what makes this video different from 10 others on the same topic?
- Does the title/thumbnail promise match what's delivered?

### Structure
| Rating | Indicators |
|--------|------------|
| Strong | Logical flow, clear sections, builds momentum |
| Weak | Rambling, confusing order, energy dips |

**Questions to ask:**
- Could a viewer outline this video from memory?
- Does each section earn its place?

---

## Script & Writing

### Hook (First 30 Seconds)
| Rating | Indicators |
|--------|------------|
| Strong | Pattern interrupt, bold claim, immediate relevance |
| Weak | Slow intro, "hey guys," unnecessary preamble |

**Patterns that work:**
- Start mid-action or mid-thought
- Lead with the outcome/transformation
- Ask a provocative question
- Show the end result first

**Patterns to avoid:**
- "So today we're going to..."
- Apologizing or hedging
- Explaining what you're about to explain

### Engagement Patterns
| Rating | Indicators |
|--------|------------|
| Strong | Open loops, callbacks, direct questions, variety |
| Weak | Monotone delivery, no interaction, predictable |

**Techniques to look for:**
- "But here's where it gets interesting..."
- Referencing something coming later
- Asking viewers to guess/predict
- Breaking the fourth wall

### CTA (Call to Action)
| Rating | Indicators |
|--------|------------|
| Strong | Natural, specific, well-timed, earns the ask |
| Weak | Forced, generic, too early, too frequent |

**Effective CTAs:**
- Tied to value just delivered
- Specific action ("comment what tool you'd try")
- Single ask, not multiple

## Delivery & Presence

### Vocal Quality
| Rating | Indicators |
|--------|------------|
| Strong | Clear, varied pace, appropriate energy, confident |
| Weak | Mumbling, monotone, too fast/slow, reading |

**Listen for:**
- Energy variation (not flat)
- Strategic pauses for emphasis
- Natural breathing, not rushed
- Clarity of pronunciation

### Eye Contact & Connection
| Rating | Indicators |
|--------|------------|
| Strong | Direct camera eye contact, conversational feel |
| Weak | Reading off-screen, looking down, darting eyes |

**Note:** Some creators effectively use looking away for emphasis—the key is intentionality.

### Gestures & Movement
| Rating | Indicators |
|--------|------------|
| Strong | Natural hand movements, purposeful positioning |
| Weak | Stiff/frozen, distracting fidgeting, hands hidden |

**Consider:**
- Does movement add energy or distract?
- Is there enough physical variation for the video length?

### Confidence Level
| Rating | Indicators |
|--------|------------|
| Strong | Speaks with authority, comfortable with silence |
| Weak | Excessive hedging, filler words, apologetic tone |

**Red flags:**
- "I think maybe..." when stating facts
- "Sorry this is long" or similar
- Undermining own expertise

## Visual Production

### Lighting
| Rating | Indicators |
|--------|------------|
| Strong | Even, flattering, consistent, intentional mood |
| Weak | Harsh shadows, unflattering angles, inconsistent |

**Basics to check:**
- Is face evenly lit?
- Any distracting shadows?
- Does lighting match the content tone?

### Background & Scene
| Rating | Indicators |
|--------|------------|
| Strong | Clean, on-brand, adds context, not distracting |
| Weak | Cluttered, random, distracting elements, messy |

**Questions:**
- Does the background communicate anything about the creator?
- Are there any unintentional distractions?
- Is depth of field used effectively?

### Framing & Composition
| Rating | Indicators |
|--------|------------|
| Strong | Rule of thirds, appropriate headroom, stable |
| Weak | Centered awkwardly, too much/little headroom, shaky |

**Check:**
- Eye line in upper third
- Appropriate negative space
- Camera at eye level or slightly above

### B-Roll & Graphics
| Rating | Indicators |
|--------|------------|
| Strong | Supports content, well-timed, high quality |
| Weak | Random stock footage, overused, poor quality |

**Questions:**
- Does every visual earn its place?
- Do graphics clarify or clutter?
- Is the style consistent?

## Engagement & Retention

### Pacing Assessment
| Video Length | Expected Pacing |
|--------------|-----------------|
| < 5 min | Fast, no wasted time, one idea |
| 5-10 min | Brisk but can breathe, clear sections |
| 10-20 min | Varied pace, deliberate slower moments |
| 20+ min | Chapter-like structure, strategic peaks |

**Red flags:**
- Long sections without visual change
- Repetition of same point
- Tangents that don't return

### The "Boring Test"
Honest assessment questions:
- At what point would an average viewer click away?
- Is there a specific section that drags?
- Would YOU watch this if you didn't make it?

### Retention Hooks
Things that keep viewers watching:
- Open loops ("I'll show you why in a minute")
- Progress indicators ("Step 3 of 5")
- Unexpected moments
- Personal stories
- Stakes and consequences

---

## Brand & Positioning

### Consistency Check
- Does this feel like the same creator as other videos?
- Is the energy/tone consistent with the brand?
- Would a subscriber recognize this without seeing the face?

### Differentiation Assessment
- What makes this creator's take unique?
- Why watch THIS video vs. a competitor's?
- Is there a distinctive voice or approach?

### Audience Building
- Does this video attract the RIGHT subscribers?
- Would watching this set accurate expectations for the channel?
- Is this expanding the audience or serving existing fans?

---

## Severity Ratings

When providing feedback, categorize issues:

**Critical** — Viewer will click away (bad audio, boring hook, unclear value)

**Important** — Reduces quality noticeably (poor lighting, weak CTA, pacing issues)

**Minor** — Polish issues (small visual distractions, occasional filler words)

**Style Choice** — Not wrong, but worth considering (could go either way)

Prioritize critical and important issues. Don't overwhelm with minor notes unless the fundamentals are solid.
