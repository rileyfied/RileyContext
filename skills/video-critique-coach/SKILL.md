# Video Critique Coach

Comprehensive feedback system for YouTube creators. Analyzes videos across multiple dimensions and provides actionable improvement recommendations.

## Workflow Overview

1. **Context Phase** — Understand creator's goals before critiquing
2. **Research Phase** — Gather current platform context
3. **Analysis Phase** — Systematic review across all dimensions
4. **Feedback Phase** — Deliver prioritized, actionable recommendations

## Phase 1: Context

Before watching/analyzing, ask 2-3 targeted questions:

**Goal clarification:**
- "What were you going for with this video?"
- "Who's the target viewer?"
- "What feeling should someone have after watching?"

**Self-assessment:**
- "What do you already think is working or not working?"
- "Any specific areas you want me to focus on?"

**Context:**
- "Is this typical of your style, or are you trying something new?"
- "How does this fit into your content strategy?"

Don't overwhelm—ask the most relevant questions based on what they share.

## Phase 2: Research

Conduct brief trend research for context. Use web_search:

- `YouTube [topic] videos trending 2026` — What's working in this space?
- `[topic] YouTube thumbnail styles` — Visual benchmarks
- `YouTube retention strategies [video length]` — Pacing expectations

Also reference `references/competitor-context.md` if the user has populated their competitor list.

Purpose: Calibrate feedback against current platform standards, not abstract ideals.

## Phase 3: Analysis

Evaluate systematically across these dimensions. See `references/critique-framework.md` for detailed criteria.

### Content & Strategy
- **Topic selection** — Relevance, timeliness, audience fit
- **Angle/hook** — Differentiation, clarity of promise
- **Structure** — Flow, pacing, logical progression
- **Value delivery** — Did it deliver on the promise?

### Script & Writing
- **Hook effectiveness** — First 30 seconds compelling?
- **Clarity** — Easy to follow? Jargon explained?
- **Engagement patterns** — Pattern interrupts, callbacks, questions
- **CTA** — Clear, natural, well-timed?

### Delivery & Presence
- **Vocal quality** — Energy, pacing, clarity, variation
- **Vocal style** — Authentic? Matches content tone?
- **Eye contact** — Connection with camera/viewer
- **Gestures/movement** — Natural? Distracting? Too static?
- **Confidence** — Believable expertise without arrogance

### Visual Production
- **Lighting** — Flattering? Consistent? Professional?
- **Background/scene** — Clean? On-brand? Distracting elements?
- **Framing** — Composition, headroom, rule of thirds
- **B-roll/graphics** — Supportive? Overused? Quality?
- **Thumbnail alignment** — Does video match thumbnail promise?

### Engagement & Retention
- **Pacing** — Too fast? Too slow? Momentum maintained?
- **Retention hooks** — Open loops, teasers, pattern interrupts
- **Boring factor** — Honest assessment: would you keep watching?
- **Rewatch value** — Any reason to return or share?

### Brand & Positioning
- **Consistency** — Matches established style?
- **Differentiation** — What makes this NOT generic content?
- **Audience building** — Does this attract the right subscribers?

## Phase 4: Feedback Delivery

Structure feedback for maximum usefulness:

### Format

**Top 3 Strengths**
What's genuinely working. Be specific, not just encouraging.

**Top 3 Priorities**
Most impactful improvements. Ranked by effort-to-impact ratio.

**Quick Wins**
Small fixes that would noticeably improve quality.

**Advanced Suggestions**
Longer-term improvements for when basics are solid.

### Delivery Guidelines

- Lead with genuine positives (not empty praise)
- Be direct about problems—kindness isn't vagueness
- Give specific timestamps when referencing moments
- Explain WHY something works or doesn't
- Offer concrete alternatives, not just criticism
- Consider the creator's current skill level
- Balance aspiration with achievability

### Comparative Context

When relevant, reference:
- How competitors handle similar content
- Current platform trends/expectations
- The creator's own previous work (if known)

## Trigger Phrases

Activate when user says:
- "Watch this and tell me what you think"
- "How can I improve this video?"
- "Critique my video"
- "What's working/not working?"
- "Give me feedback on..."
- "What would you change?"
- "Is this boring?"
- Any variation of video review/feedback requests
