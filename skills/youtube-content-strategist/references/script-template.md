# Script Template

## Script Structure

### 1. Hook (0:00 - 0:30)
The first 30 seconds determine whether someone keeps watching. Options:

**Pattern Interrupt**
Start with something unexpected that breaks the scroll.
> "I just saved 4 hours on scheduling—and I didn't touch a spreadsheet."

**Bold Claim**
Make a promise that creates curiosity.
> "By the end of this video, you'll have a system that writes your weekly reports automatically."

**Relatable Problem**
Name the pain point directly.
> "If you've ever spent your Sunday night dreading the week ahead, this is for you."

**Contrarian Take**
Challenge conventional wisdom.
> "Everyone's using AI wrong. Here's what actually works."

### 2. Context (0:30 - 1:30)
Establish why this matters NOW. Include:
- Why you're making this video
- Who this is for (be specific)
- What they'll be able to do after watching
- Brief credibility moment (keep it humble)

### 3. Core Content (1:30 - End minus 1:00)
The actual tutorial/demo/commentary. Structure options:

**For Tutorials:**
1. Show the end result first (motivation)
2. Walk through setup (keep it tight)
3. Demonstrate the workflow
4. Show a real example
5. Troubleshoot common issues

**For Commentary:**
1. State your position clearly
2. Present the strongest counterargument
3. Give your reasoning
4. Provide nuance/caveats
5. Land on an actionable takeaway

**For Demos:**
1. Show the problem (before state)
2. Introduce the solution
3. Walk through the process
4. Show the result (after state)
5. Discuss variations and limitations

### 4. Callback & CTA (Final 30-60 seconds)
End strong:
- Summarize the ONE key takeaway
- Specific call to action (not just "subscribe")
- Tease related content
- End with energy (not trailing off)

---

## Script Format

Write scripts in this format for easy recording:

```
[HOOK]
"Opening line goes here. Bold, direct, no warmup."

[CONTEXT]
Today I'm going to show you [specific outcome].
This is for [specific person] who [specific situation].
By the end, you'll [specific capability].

[SECTION 1: Setup]
First, let's [action].
[Visual note: show screen with X]

[SECTION 2: Main Process]
Now here's where it gets interesting...
[Visual note: zoom on specific element]

[SECTION 3: Results]
And look at what we get...
[Visual note: before/after comparison]

[CTA]
If you want to [related outcome], check out [next video].
Drop a comment telling me [specific question].
```

---

## Pacing Guidelines

- **Shorts/TikTok (< 60 sec)**: One idea, one demonstration, immediate payoff
- **Short-form (5-8 min)**: Single focused tutorial or take
- **Standard (10-15 min)**: Tutorial with context + troubleshooting
- **Deep-dive (20+ min)**: Comprehensive guide or multi-part workflow

---

## Voice Notes

### Do:
- Talk like you're explaining to a smart friend
- Use "you" and "we" not "one" or "users"
- Include moments of genuine reaction
- Acknowledge when something is confusing

### Don't:
- Use AI/tech jargon without explaining it
- Rush through important steps
- Apologize for video length
- End with "anyway, that's it"
