# Brand Identity

## Core Mission
Help non-technical people understand how to use AI in their daily lives—moving them from "chat thread" mode to "systems and automation" mode.

## Target Audience

### Primary: Frontline Operational Managers
- Restaurant managers, retail supervisors, construction foremen, healthcare shift leads
- Roles involving embodied work and in-person employee engagement
- Real-time problem-solving, not long-horizon knowledge work
- Limited time at screens, need solutions that work fast
- Skeptical of tech hype, need practical proof

### Secondary: Non-Tech Knowledge Workers
- People who use computers but don't code
- Overwhelmed by AI noise, don't know where to start
- Want to be more efficient but don't want to become "AI people"

## Brand Voice

### Tone
- **Accessible**: No jargon, no assumptions about technical background
- **Practical**: Every video answers "what can I actually DO with this?"
- **Honest**: Acknowledge limitations, don't oversell AI capabilities
- **Warm**: Inviting, not intimidating; curious, not condescending
- **Grounded**: Real examples, real use cases, real results

### What We DON'T Sound Like
- Tech bro hype ("This changes EVERYTHING")
- Corporate training video (sterile, generic)
- Clickbait panic ("Your job is GONE")
- Condescending explainer ("Let me break this down for you")

## Content Philosophy

### The Shift We're Teaching
FROM: "I asked ChatGPT a question and got an answer"
TO: "I built a system that handles this automatically"

### Content Priorities
1. **Immediate utility** — Can someone use this TODAY?
2. **Repeatability** — Can they do this themselves after watching?
3. **Scalability** — Does this save more time than it takes to learn?
4. **Accessibility** — Can someone with zero coding experience follow along?

### Video Types That Fit the Brand
- Step-by-step tutorials with real-world scenarios
- "Watch me solve a real problem" demonstrations
- Honest tool reviews (what works, what doesn't)
- "AI for your role" series targeting specific jobs
- Myth-busting and expectation-setting content

### Video Types That DON'T Fit
- News reaction videos with no practical takeaway
- "Top 10 AI tools" listicles with no depth
- Hype pieces about AGI or far-future speculation
- Content that requires coding to follow along

## Visual Identity

### Aesthetic
- Clean, classic, design-forward
- Not generic AI imagery (no robot hands, no matrix code)
- Warm colors over cold tech blues
- Real environments over abstract backgrounds

### Thumbnail Style
- Bold, readable text (3-4 words max)
- Clear visual hierarchy
- Avoid busy compositions
- Face when appropriate (builds trust)

## The Excitement Filter

Every video idea must pass this test:
1. Would I genuinely enjoy making this? (Not just "should I" but "do I want to")
2. Would I watch this if I weren't the creator?
3. Does this teach something I wish I'd known earlier?
4. Can I bring a unique perspective or approach?

If any answer is no, the idea needs refinement or rejection.
